# Claude Cowork Workshop — Attendee Materials

Welcome! This is a **hands-on, three-part series** introducing **Claude Cowork** — using Claude not just to *chat*, but to *do real work* for you: your files, your meetings, your documents.

**Who it's for:** non-technical Visma folks — QAs, Business Analysts, Product Managers. **No coding needed.**

**The promise, in plain terms:**
> "It does my fiddly document and meeting work, the way I'd do it — and it's waiting for me in the morning."

You don't need to prepare anything except the [Preflight](#preflight-do-this-before-part-1) below. Each part has its own folder with the goal, the practical plan, and a short plain-language theory recap.

## The three parts

| Part | Folder | What you'll be able to do by the end |
|---|---|---|
| **Part 1 — Foundations** | [`Part 1 - Foundations/`](./Part%201%20-%20Foundations/) | Have Claude do a real, fiddly **file job** for you — in a project that's yours, and that only ever touches what you allow. |
| **Part 2 — Furnishing the home** | [`Part 2 - Furnishing the home/`](./Part%202%20-%20Furnishing%20the%20home/) | Turn a **messy meeting transcript** into the next version of a real **requirements document** — and have the project *remember* your work. |
| **Part 3 — Going further** | [`Part 3 - Going further/`](./Part%203%20-%20Going%20further/) | Turn a repeatable job into a reusable **skill**, get a **live** result, put Claude on a **schedule**, and build one real flow end-to-end yourself. |

## Three ideas we keep coming back to

1. **Outcome-oriented prompting** — describe the *outcome you want*, not the step-by-step clicks. (Outcome-oriented isn't vague: for real work you still state the scope and the source of truth.)
2. **You stay in control** — Claude only touches what you allow, and you can change or revoke that any time. The more reach *and* independence it has, the more deliberate you are about what you allow.
3. **The project is your one home** — everything you set up lives in the *project*, not the chat. A session is one visit; the project stays furnished — and it remembers across sessions.

## Preflight (do this before Part 1)

Confirm on your own laptop, the day before:
- [ ] **Claude desktop app** installed and updated (the installed app, not the website).
- [ ] **Signed in** with your Visma account (complete any SSO prompt).
- [ ] You can find the control that **switches the app into Cowork mode**.
- [ ] You've copied the **demo folder** your facilitator shares to somewhere easy to find (e.g. your Desktop).

If anything doesn't work, tell your facilitator *before* the day — we won't have time to fix installs in the session.

## Two ground rules

- **Data rule:** on the shared screen, only ever use the sample data. On your own screen your real data is fine — just don't read customer details aloud.
- **No-shame rule:** some steps use features your admin controls (certain connectors and plugins). If a button isn't available to you, you'll watch that part — that's expected, not a failure. There are catch-up points throughout; raise a hand any time.

## A note on what's available to you

Claude Cowork is evolving, and some features are **switched on by your organisation**, not by you — especially **connectors** (links to Google Drive, Calendar, and similar) and **plugins**. Where a step depends on one of these, the material says so. If it's not enabled for you yet, follow along on the shared screen — the idea still lands.
