# Part 1 — Foundations: your Cowork home

## What you'll leave with
By the end of this part you'll have **Claude do a real, fiddly file job for you** — and you'll know it only ever touches what you allow. You'll have your own **project** (your home base), you'll have set how cautious Claude should be, and you'll have tidied a messy folder just by describing the result you wanted.

## Short theory (plain language)

**What Claude Cowork is.** An AI assistant you can chat with **and hand real work to** — it can read and write your files and carry out multi-step tasks for you. The way it works, in four steps:

1. **You describe the outcome** you want (not the steps).
2. **Claude plans** and breaks the job down.
3. **It works in its own safe space** — scoped to the folder you point it at.
4. **It delivers** — files, results, answers.

**Two things you steer with *any* AI** (worth knowing before anything else):
- **Its short-term attention is limited.** Like a colleague in one very long meeting, a single chat that runs forever and covers everything starts to lose the thread. Keep one chat focused on one job; start a fresh chat for a new job.
- **Always review what it produces.** Claude is very capable but can be *confidently wrong*. You stay the editor — and the bigger the task, the more the review matters.

**Project vs session.** A **project** is a persistent home tied to a folder — it holds your files, your rules, your background, and it **remembers your work across sessions**. A **session** is a single chat. Think of the project as the room and a session as one visit: you furnish the room once, and every visit benefits.

**The safe little computer.** Claude does its file work in its own separate, locked space — like a single desk drawer, not the keys to the whole office. It can only open the folder(s) you authorize, and you can grant or revoke that any time. *(One honest caveat: if you ask Claude to drive other apps on your screen, that part isn't locked down the same way — keep those tasks supervised.)*

**You stay in control.** Cowork has built-in approval controls and **always asks before permanently deleting files**. You can write a standing rule (a "global instruction" — a house rule you write once and Claude follows in every Cowork session) to extend that same caution to overwriting and renaming.

## What you'll do (practical)

> Prompts you'll type are shown in quotes. You don't need to prepare them — they're here so you can follow along and reuse them afterwards.

1. **Switch to Cowork mode** and type one throwaway line to get a feel for it, e.g.
   > "Say hello and tell me what folder you can see."

2. **Create your project** — use **"Work in a project"** and create a new project pointed at your copied demo folder.
   - ✅ Done when the app shows you're working *in* your new project, scoped to the demo folder.

3. **Set how cautious Claude is** — set it to *ask before acting*, then add this standing rule:
   > "Always ask for my confirmation before deleting, overwriting, or renaming any files."
   - ✅ Done when your approval setting is ask-first and your rule is saved.

4. **Your first real task — organize a folder.** On the messy demo folder, type:
   > "Organize this folder — put the files into sub-folders by type: PDFs, spreadsheets, images, and so on."
   Let Claude propose the groupings and move the files — approve when it asks.
   - ✅ Done when there are several type-named sub-folders, no loose files, and you saw Claude pause for your approval before moving anything.

5. **Now write your own.** Same folder, *your* wording, a different goal — e.g. *"sort these by how recently they were changed"* or *"group anything related to the transaction list together."* This is the real skill: you describe the outcome, Claude works out the how.
   - ✅ Done when you wrote the prompt yourself (not copied) and got a sensible result.

## What you need
- The **demo folder** of mixed files (PDFs, spreadsheets, images, documents…) that your facilitator shares — copy it somewhere local before the session.
